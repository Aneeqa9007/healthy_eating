Lentil patties
Makes 25 patties

Ingredients

  1. 2 cups dry red lentils, rinsed & soaked overnight
  2. 1 white onion, diced
  3. 3 cloves garlic, minced
  4. 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  5. Â½ tablespoon chickpea flour
  6. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  7. 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  8. 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  9. Sea salt & pepper to taste
  10. 4-5 tablespoons olive oil for frying

Procedure

1. Rinse and soak the red lentils in enough water to cover the top layer. They will expand, so be generous with the amount of water you use. Soaking them overnight is recommended, but if not, then for at least 2-3 hours. 2. After the lentils have soaked, drain and add them to a food processor along with the onion, garlic, tomato paste, chickpea flour, and seasonings. Pulse everything together until you have a smooth batter (it's okay if there are some chunks, or if it seems a little runny). 3. Next, heat the olive or grapeseed oil in a frying pan on medium. You'll know the oil is hot enough when you stick a wooden handle in it, and you see bubbles around it. 4. Form 1-2 tablespoon patties in your hands, flattening them slightly. Fry them on each side for 2-3 minutes, or until golden brown. 5. Transfer each patty to a plate lined with paper towel to absorb excess oil. 6. Serve hot with dip or sauce of your choice.

Nutritional Information

  • Serving size: 2 patties
  • Calories: 158 kcal
  • Carbohydrates: 21.2 g
  • Protein: 8 g
  • Fats: 5 g
