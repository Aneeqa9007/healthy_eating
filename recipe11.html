Saffron Chicken Kebabs with Salad
(Serves 6)

Ingredients

  1. 1 kg boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into 2-inch cubes
  2. 1 pinch saffron threads (about 10 threads) or ¼ teaspoon of ground saffron
  3. 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  4. 1 large garlic clove, crushed or minced
  5. Juice from 1 large lemon
  6. ⅓ cup Greek yogurt
  7. 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  8. 1 tablespoon tomato purée
  9. ½ teaspoon turmeric
  10. Pink salt or Himalayan salt
  11. Black pepper
  12. Finely chopped coriander, for garnish

    13. For Salad:

  13. 6 tomatoes, diced (about 3 cups diced tomatoes)
  14. 2 large cucumbers, diced
  15. ½ small onion, thinly sliced, optional
  16. ¾ bunch mint leaves, leaves and tender stems chopped (about ½-3/4 cup chopped leaves )
  17. Pink salt, to taste
  18. ½ teaspoon black pepper
  19. 1 teaspoon ground Sumac (optional)
  20. 2 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  21. 2 teaspoon freshly squeezed lemon juice, plus more to taste

Procedure

1. Use either a pestle and mortar or a spice grinder to grind the saffron until all of the strands become a fine powder. 2. Stir ¼ teaspoon of the ground saffron with 3 tablespoons of water. Allow to bloom for 10 minutes. 3. To a large bowl, add the sliced onion, garlic, lemon juice, yogurt, olive oil, tomato puree, turmeric, and the bloomed saffron water. Stir to combine. 4. Place the chicken into the bowl with the marinade and season with a large pinch of salt and pepper. Massage the marinade into the chicken until everything is combined and evenly distributed. 5. Cover with plastic wrap and leave in the fridge overnight for the flavors to develop. 6. The next day, remove chicken from the fridge, shake off any excess marinade and thread the chicken pieces onto the soaked skewers. (Discard the marinade and onions.) 7. Lightly oil and heat a gas grill or griddle to medium-high heat. Add the kebabs and cook, uncovered, until the chicken is cooked through, about 10-12 minutes in total. 8. Place the cooked Kebabs on a serving dish and sprinkle with finely chopped fresh coriander. 9. To make the salad, gently combine the tomatoes, cucumbers, and parsley. Season with kosher salt in a large mixing bowl and toss. 10. Set aside for 5 minutes. 11. Add the sumac, olive oil, and lemon juice. Give the salad a gentle toss. Taste and adjust the seasoning to your liking. 12. Serve the kebabs and salad with rice and a yogurt dip.

Nutritional Information

  • Calories: 318.7 kcal
  • Carbohydrates: 10 g
  • Protein: 38.7 g
  • Fat: 12.5 g
